unlikely, maybe Bruno Pernadas

rating: ★★★★☆

mark:

Untitled (raindrops) Juro que vi túlipas (feat. Maya Blandy) Já não tem mais encanto (feat. Lívia Nestrovski) Spiritual Spaceman (feat. Leonor Arnaut) Leo Minor (feat. Leonor Arnaut & Margarida Campelo) Song in MT-65 (feat. José Soares)

This is a Jazz Fusion work by Bruno Pernadas, executed with an almost relaxed yet highly conscious approach. Amidst the intricate yet not overcrowded layering, he constantly shifts unconventional rhythmic patterns, complemented by streamlined brass arrangements and lush, rhythmic electronic fills. Despite such a complex structure, the overall listening experience remains light and vibrant. It is worth noting that this album reorganizes many slightly retro indie rock idioms and effectively updates them contextually—even if the source material might be criticized as somewhat commonplace, this re-integration of existing elements is precisely the most dynamic aspect of fusion aesthetics. (By the way, track 4 features the hallucinatory female vocals reminiscent of Stereolab’s lead singer.)

这是Bruno Pernadas以一种近乎松弛却高度自觉的姿态完成的Jazz Fusion作品。在极繁却不显拥挤的层次堆叠之中，他不断切换非常规节奏型，辅以流线化的管乐编排与华丽而富有律动的电子填充；如此复杂的结构之下，整体却依然维持着轻盈、明快的听感。值得注意的是，这张专辑将诸多略显复古的独立摇滚语汇重新组织，并在语境上实现了有效更新——即便可能被质疑素材取径略显常见，但这种对既有元素的再整合，本身正是fusion美学中最具张力的部分。（btw track 4的女声幻听stereolab主唱）
