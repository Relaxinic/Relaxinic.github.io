Luminescent Creatures

无需任何人工的现代化设备，仅以最本真的人类肌肤来接触海洋本源的原初气息，你一头坠入浓郁深厚的蔚蓝。缓缓沉底时的沉寂被零星的发光生灵所扰动，细碎的发光生物如漂浮的音节般在周围游曳，你望见了远处轮廓若隐若现的鲸群。鲸群的共鸣在海底回荡，深邃又空灵，抚平时间的褶皱；顷刻间，光的微粒逐渐聚拢，如同波浪般发出微弱的荧光。每个孤独的浮游生物以光为语，编织出一片无声的交流，构成一场无声的庆典，深海似乎并不是你想象般的沉寂，它的沉默有着重量，那种无声之中的层叠喧嚣，那种在静谧中迸发的灵动生气。

Without any modern equipment, relying solely on the most primal human skin to touch the original essence of the ocean, you plunge headfirst into the deep, rich blue. The silence of your slow descent is disturbed by scattered luminescent creatures, tiny glowing organisms drifting like floating syllables around you. In the distance, you glimpse the faint outlines of a whale pod. Their resonant calls echo through the depths, profound and ethereal, smoothing the wrinkles of time. In an instant, particles of light gradually gather, emitting a faint glow like waves. Each solitary plankton speaks through light, weaving a silent exchange that forms a wordless celebration. The deep sea is not as silent as you imagined—its silence carries weight, a layered clamor within the quiet, a vibrant vitality bursting forth in the stillness.

青葉市子的正是在这样的意象中诞生。延续自的海岛灵感，这张专辑不再只是对冲绳诸岛的声响纪实，而是将海洋意象拓展至更加多维的诗性空间音乐语言上，专辑依旧以Chamber Folk为骨架，但即使调动的是管弦乐队的宏大编制，青葉市子的编排也依旧显得轻盈，恰到好处的留白让想象在空隙中萌生。

Ichiko Aoba’s was born from such imagery. Building on the island-inspired sounds of , this album is no longer just an auditory documentary of Okinawa’s islands but expands the oceanic imagery into a more multidimensional, poetic musical language. The album still uses chamber folk as its backbone, yet even when employing the grand arrangements of an orchestra, Aoba’s compositions remain light and airy, with just the right amount of negative space to let imagination flourish in the gaps.

在作词上，青葉市子依旧选择弱化文字的存在来深化静谧氛围，散文化的抽象表达碎片化地将整个空间营造出近乎完美的意境，诗意化的文笔也将专辑蕴含的美学阐释地更加到位。这种处理方式在听觉上模糊了意义的边界，却恰恰放大了情感的张力，使得整张专辑的氛围几乎脱离语义，直指听觉与情绪的原始交界。

Lyrically, Aoba continues to minimize the presence of words to deepen the tranquil atmosphere. Fragmented, abstract, prose-like expressions create a near-perfect spatial ambiance, while her poetic writing further elucidates the album’s aesthetic. This approach blurs the boundaries of meaning in the auditory experience but amplifies emotional tension, allowing the album’s atmosphere to transcend semantics and directly engage the primal intersection of sound and emotion.

青葉市子那如水晶般剔透的嗓音以及和谐的合声更是专辑中值得称道的一大优点。相较于，她对歌曲情感传达效果的处理又上了一层，尤其在如、<Luciférine>等段落中，空灵的哼鸣与细腻的咬字使得专辑的意境烘托得更加到位。

Aoba’s crystal-clear voice and harmonious backing vocals are another standout feature of the album. Compared to , her handling of emotional delivery has reached new heights, particularly in tracks like and <Luciférine>, where ethereal hums and delicate enunciation elevate the album’s mood even further.

专辑当中海洋的各种意象作为整张专辑的叙事语法，通过多种方式来传达——有<24° 3′ 27.0″ N, 123° 47′ 7.5″ E>当中对波照间岛的Mushama祭典民谣的哼唱，海洋与人类的肌肤相亲在三线的弹奏下显得美好而宁静；有中对鲸鸣的采样与更进一步的细节处理，加之晶莹剔透的铃声，轻柔的海风仿佛拂过耳朵；更不用说鲸的意象在歌词中被多次提及……

Oceanic imagery serves as the album’s narrative grammar, conveyed in multiple ways—through the hummed folk melodies of the Mushama festival in <24° 3′ 27.0″ N, 123° 47′ 7.5″ E>, where the intimacy between ocean and human skin feels serene and beautiful under the strumming of the sanshin; through the sampled whale calls and intricate detailing in , paired with crystalline chimes that make the gentle sea breeze seem to brush past your ears; not to mention the recurring motif of whales in the lyrics…

在编曲制作上，青葉市子也依旧保持着曾经的高水准。开场曲中通过长笛、钢琴、人声的交相辉映描绘出勃勃生机，弦乐铺底的华丽开场在收尾中节奏放缓，仿佛跃进水中，缓缓坠入海底，氛围也随之变得奇幻神秘，音景从空气转入液态。中有关鲸鸣的采样配上优秀流畅的作曲将听众从下坠中唤醒，轻柔的合成器音色也显得毫不突兀，反而轻轻将听感托起，巧妙地填补了听觉中的空白，仿佛身处一片鲸群当中。是整张专辑中旋律线编写的最为优秀的一首，从简约而不简单的钢琴铺底到第一次副歌中人声与器乐的巧妙配合，钢琴的分解和弦与灵动的提琴拨奏犹如浪潮般赋予歌曲以灵动感，优雅的华尔兹节奏也被海洋的神秘氛围所拥抱，化作阵阵波浪。中吉他的演奏仿佛在模拟波光粼粼的海面，配上洁净的人声，海的澄澈已不再止于眼前，而是深入人心。<Luciférine>的作曲更是从和弦走向开始就不一般，我们跟随乐曲经历了柳暗花明迂回婉转，最后终于见到生机迸发的繁荣，感受生命的华丽。在整张专辑中是难得的电子化尝试，在并不破坏氛围的同时通过对声呐的听觉模拟，补全了海洋意境的另一个碎片，拓展出一层新的空间维度。<惑星の泪>作为本张专辑的收尾，不仅是最旋律化的一首，也是情感最为真诚的一首。通过风声铺底，回归最朴素的民谣姿态，整张专辑也归于本源。

In terms of arrangement and production, Aoba maintains her high standards. The opening track, , paints a vivid scene with the interplay of flute, piano, and vocals, its lush orchestral introduction slowing in tempo toward the end, as if diving into water and sinking slowly into the depths, the atmosphere turning fantastical and mysterious—the soundscape shifting from air to liquid. awakens listeners from their descent with its fluid composition and whale-call samples, while the soft synth tones feel seamless, gently lifting the listening experience and filling the auditory gaps as if surrounded by a pod of whales. features the album’s most exquisite melodic writing, from its deceptively simple piano foundation to the masterful interplay between vocals and instruments in the first chorus. The piano’s arpeggios and the playful plucking of strings lend the song a wave-like fluidity, while the elegant waltz rhythm is embraced by the ocean’s mysterious aura, transforming into rolling waves. In , the guitar seems to mimic the shimmering surface of the sea, paired with pristine vocals that make the ocean’s clarity not just visible but deeply felt. <Luciférine> stands out from its chord progressions alone, taking listeners through twists and turns before finally revealing a burst of vitality, a celebration of life’s splendor. is a rare electronic experiment on the album, simulating sonar sounds without disrupting the atmosphere, completing another fragment of the oceanic imagery and expanding into a new spatial dimension. <惑星の泪 (Planet’s Tears)>, the album’s closing track, is not only the most melodic but also the most emotionally sincere. With wind sounds as its foundation, it returns to the simplest folk form, bringing the entire album back to its origins.

这是一场关于海底梦境的纪实。光、深海、生物、孤独、语言，音乐在此时近乎全然摆脱了人类中心主义，在信息流过载的当下构建出非叙事的真实一隅，缓慢却辽阔，模糊却动人。

This is a documentary of an underwater dream. Light, the deep sea, creatures, solitude, language—here, the music nearly escapes anthropocentrism, constructing a non-narrative, truthful corner in an era of information overload. Slow yet vast, ambiguous yet deeply moving.
